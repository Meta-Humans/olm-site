Open Learning Map

A simple public project to make learning easier to describe, share, and improve — without forcing it into one model.

Early / in development Public overview

What it is

Open Learning Map (OLM) is a lightweight way to talk about learning as it actually happens — across different environments, people, and rhythms.

It is being developed as an open project, with clarity and usefulness as the primary goals.

What it isn’t

  • Not a curriculum
  • Not a school replacement
  • Not a certification or credential
  • Not a rigid framework for ranking learners

Why it exists

People often know when learning is working, but struggle to describe it clearly enough to support it, repeat it, or collaborate around it.

OLM exists to make that kind of clarity easier.

Where it is today

  • A minimal public landing page
  • Ongoing work on language and practical artifacts
  • More public material as concepts stabilize
